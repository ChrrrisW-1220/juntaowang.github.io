Research Experiences

Prediction of Wireless Network Traffic in High-Dimensional Beam Space

CUHK-Shenzhen & SRIBD & HUAWEI Innovation Laboratory for Future Network Systems Optimization

  • This research project was led by Prof. Zhiquan (Tom) Luo and Prof. Feng Yin.

  • Our research aimed to accurately predict wireless network traffic in the high-dimensional beam space formed by large-scale directional antennas for the new generation wireless communication system. An accurate prediction can help better tune the wireless network system parameters to meet future traffic demands, increase signal coverage, improve resource utilization efficiency, and enhance user experience.

  • The challenge of this project was the complexity of WN traffic data in the HDBS (e.g., noise, no physical spatial location mapping, signal overlapping, and poor predictability) so it is very challenging to achieve accurate prediction. To this end, we proposed a model combining a high-dimensional data clustering module and spatio-temporal prediction module.

  • My work focused on the temporal prediction submodule, including deep learning model Long Short-term Memory (LSTM) and its combination with Graph Neural Network (GNN). I constructed the main framework of the temporal prediction submodule called Multi-LSTM Attention algorithm, which included Attention Mechanism, LSTM, and GAN.

  • Thanks to CUHK-Shenzhen Undergraduate Research Award (URA) and research assistant reward from SRIBD for sponsoring me to conduct this research.

 

Graph Neural Network for Large-Scale Network Localization

  • I recently joined in the research of Professor Yin’s Ph.D. student Mr. Wenzhong Yan. This research is an extend the research of Mr. Yan’s ICASSP conference paper. We aim to solve large-scale network localization problems using GNN-class models. In our previous research, we adopted GCN for a classic but challenging nonlinear regression problem, i.e. the network localization. And we found that the proposed GNN based method outperforms all benchmarks by far. In terms of good performance of GNN under this scenario, we want to further explore in this field.

  • So far, we have acquired some remarkable results and we are expanding this conference paper into a journal paper with deeper insights and more innovations…
